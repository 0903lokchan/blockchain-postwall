Rosetta on Chain

Throughout human history, people try to make long-lasting records of important messages: knots, engravings, books, disks. Leaving messages for the future is a core value of humanity, yet none of the attempts succeeds.

Blockchain is a breakthrough in human recording technologies. With the aid of it, a new way to store critical information is found. Rosetta on Chain is an interface built on top of a set of algorithms residing on a public blockchain, that saves messages of yours on a publicly owned domain. This makes the data you upload to be imperishable... as long as the Internet stands.

How does it work?

Rosetta on Chain is a prototype of a new form of data storage. It is not only a web application. It interacts with a publicly owned set of algorithms on a blockchain that does no more than storing and serving data its users upload. It ensures that all the messages from the users are kept original and untouched.

What Blockchain really is? How does it help?

Blockchain is a kind of computer networks that collaborates in as swarm intelligence. That means every worker in the network plays an equal role and there is no centre of the network. In the use of data storage, it means that the data stored on it is securely distributed. Every bit of data will remain permanent and immutable as long as the blockchain survives.

What purpose is this of?

Rosetta on Chain is a perfect solution for leaving important messages to the public, as your announcements will not be altered, censored or perished. Rosetta on Chain also ensures wide reach of your messages since it in default broadcasts every post to every user. The toll fee nature of blockchain systems also encourages users on Rosetta on Chain to put important messages on the blockchain, so you will not be flooded by insignificant daily posts.
